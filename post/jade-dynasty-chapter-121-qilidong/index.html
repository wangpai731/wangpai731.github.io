One after another, houses with a strong flavor of Miao people have sprung up from the ground, or built in the mountains, or closely connected. There is also a clear stream, originated in the mountains ahead, from this piece of land in general, winding through. Many houses of the Miao people were built on both sides of the stream.

On the surface of the water, from a distance, the Miao people have built three bridges, which are all different. One is a wooden bridge. The simplest one is a wooden bridge. Two giant trees are tied together and fall on both sides of the river, so they can be regarded as a bridge.

As for the other two, they are both stone bridges, but they have more flavor. A large stone, rough and solid, laid horizontally on a stream with a narrow water surface, and then built with heavy stone slabs, is a bridge, which is a simple and practical way to build bridges here in southern Xinjiang.

But the last stone bridge. It is made of small stones, and there is no pier. It is an arch bridge, and each stone is closely connected. It’s really strange that the bridge flavor of the middle earth area can be seen here when we fly across the stream.

I see these in the background of my eyes. The heart moved, but the face did not show. He and Xiaobai continued to walk forward, and the crowd became more and more lively. Most of them don’t understand the language.

He nodded his head slightly and looked around. At the moment, they are walking among the Miao people. This road is on the side of the stream. On one side are the houses closely connected by the Miao people. On the other side is the clear river. There are many green trees on the bank.

At first glance, Miao people’s houses are mostly made of wood. The style is quite different from that of the Middle Earth. It is square and simple. There are ferocious animal bones hanging on the front door and the edge wall of the house. The more fierce the beast is, the more common it is. I think this is mostly the custom of Miao people to show the bravery of the house owner.

He has been to southern Xinjiang many times in the past years. Although he did not pay close attention to the customs of Southern Xinjiang because he was full of worries, he still knew some basic things.

In the border area of Southern Xinjiang, Zhuang, Miao, Tu, Li and Gaoshan are divided into different regions, or they believe in different gods and religions because of their different tribes. However, among all ethnic groups, there is a special place for sacrificing the ancestors of gods, which is the altar.

In fact, the altar has a high status among the tribes in southern Xinjiang. In the eyes of most tribal people, most of the time, there is no difference between the words of witches in the altar and those of great and mysterious gods. In the past, when the struggle among the five ethnic groups was most fierce, each war had to consult the wizard in the altar, explain the situation to the God, and get the permission of the God, that is, the great wizard himself, so that the clan leader could launch a new war.

From this we can see what kind of status the altar and the witches in it have in southern Xinjiang!
